Pili

A calm connection system for older adults, families and caregivers.

Pili helps older adults stay connected through simple everyday moments, photos, voice notes, quick replies, reminders, and reassurance, without requiring them to manage a complex device.

Timeline
March – May 2026
Role
UX Research, Product Design, UI Direction, Physical Prototyping
Team
Junchao & Niharika
Tools
Figma, 3D Printing, Physical Prototyping
UX Research Product Design UI Direction Physical Prototyping
Overview

What is Pili?

Pili is a calm connection system designed for older adults living apart from family, especially in care environments such as assisted living, nursing homes, and hospitals.

Pili combines three touchpoints: a tabletop device for older adults, a family app for sending moments, and a caregiver dashboard for care environments.

Pili Device

A tabletop device for older adults, with simple physical buttons, calm messaging, and visible reassurance.

Family App

Lets family members send photos, notes, and quick check-ins without making the older adult manage another app.

Caregiver Dashboard

Helps care staff understand resident status, support requests, and room-level information.

The Challenge

The Problem

Regular contact does not always mean emotional closeness. For many older adults, communication tools can feel overwhelming, and asking for support can feel difficult or disruptive.

The Why

Why it Matters

The issue is not only loneliness. It is the gap between wanting to feel connected and having tools that feel simple, calm, and appropriate for older adults, families, and caregivers.

Research Findings

User Needs

  1. 01

    Feel remembered without having to ask for attention

  2. 02

    Receive family updates without navigating complex apps

  3. 03

    Ask for support without feeling watched or monitored

  4. 04

    Trust the device through simple, familiar interaction

Digital Interfaces

Designing the Digital Touchpoints

While the physical device reduces interaction complexity for the older adult, the supporting apps help family members and caregivers send care, monitor updates, and respond when needed.

Family App

The family app helps relatives send photos, short messages, and quick emotional check-ins to the older adult’s Pili device.

Send PhotoSend MessageSend “Thinking of You”Message History

Caregiver Dashboard

The caregiver dashboard helps staff view resident profiles, care requests, and room-level information without turning the system into a surveillance tool.

Resident OverviewCare RequestsRoom InfoQuick Access
Physical Making

Form Exploration

I explored multiple physical directions before arriving at a calm, visible, approachable tabletop device.

The final form needed to feel stable, familiar, and easy to understand. I tested circular companion objects, softer rounded forms, and finally a sloped tabletop direction with a screen, physical buttons, and a grounded base.

Version 1

Circular companion form

A soft, familiar object language inspired by clocks, speakers, and tabletop objects.

Version 2

Softer rounded object

A more minimal form with fewer visual cues and a calmer silhouette.

Version 3

Final direction

A sloped tabletop form with a clear screen angle, large physical buttons, and a stable base.

SCAD Senior Showcase · 2026

Exhibited at the SCAD Senior Show

Pili was presented at the SCAD senior showcase as a complete product and experience system. The exhibit included the physical prototype, interaction cards, app touchpoints, and process documentation.

Outcome

Outcome

Pili became a complete connection system spanning physical product design, mobile UI, caregiver tools, prototyping, and exhibition storytelling.

The project helped me practice designing across emotional needs, accessibility, physical interaction, and service ecosystems.

Design Your Lab

An AI-assisted layout planning tool that helps engineers turn early client requirements into clearer 2D lab system layouts before high-effort modeling begins.

Concise case study · AI-assisted UX prototype

Project Type
AI / UX Web App
Timeline
2026
Role
UX Research, Product Strategy, Interface Design, AI-assisted Prototyping
Tools
Figma, React basics, Claude, GitHub
Overview

Planning before production

BioSpherix Layout Planner is a web-based planning prototype designed to help engineers organize early client requirements, reference system components, and create clearer 2D lab layouts before moving into CAD or Blender.

The Problem

Where the workflow slowed down

The early planning process relied on repeated manual interpretation, scattered component references, and time-consuming layout revisions. The goal was not to replace the engineer’s expertise, but to give them a clearer starting point before production-level modeling.

My Role

What I contributed

I translated the engineer’s workflow into a structured interface, mapped how client requirements could become planning inputs, and used AI-assisted prototyping to test how a layout tool could support faster early-stage decision-making.

Prototype Feedback

From engineer feedback to interface requirements

Prototype review sessions helped translate the engineer’s workflow into specific interface requirements: adding and removing components from the library, editing component card details, introducing new categories, changing the language from bench configuration to system configuration, and replacing placeholder branding with the BioSpherix identity.

Key Design Decisions

Designing for engineering clarity

  • Turn client requirements into a structured planning flow
  • Create a visual layout space before high-effort rendering
  • Keep the interface technical, calm, and easy to scan
  • Position AI as a planning assistant while keeping final layout decisions with the engineer
Working Prototype

Building the layout planning workflow

The working prototype organized components into a left-side library, a scalable floor grid, and a live readout panel for footprint, chamber count, gas requirements, weight, and pricing. The goal was to make early layout planning easier to scan before moving into high-effort CAD or Blender modeling.

Output

Turning the layout into a proposal

The print proposal translated the configured layout into a shareable document, carrying the floor plan, selected components, live readout, gas requirements, and pricing into a format that could support early client communication.

Outcome

Outcome

The project became a functional AI-assisted planning prototype that translated engineer feedback into a clearer layout workflow, connecting component selection, floor planning, live system readouts, and printable proposal output.

Wisdom ATL Eyewear

A three-frame eyewear, packaging, visual merchandising, and retail storytelling system.

This collaboration translated Wisdom ATL’s surreal, playful, and premium brand language into a buyer-facing eyewear system spanning frame families, physical prototyping, packaging, campaign thinking, merchandising, and lookbook direction.

Project Type
Product, Packaging & Visual Merchandising
Timeline
2026
Role
Product Concepting, Packaging Design, Visual Merchandising, Retail Pitch
Tools
Figma, Adobe Suite, 3D Visualization, Physical Prototyping
Overview

The collaboration

Wisdom ATL is an Atlanta-based artwear brand known for surreal design, cultural nuance, and a balance between stoic identity and childlike attitude. The project asked us to extend that world into eyewear, packaging, merchandising, and retail storytelling.

Challenge

Bold, but retail-ready

Create eyewear concepts that felt bold enough to stand apart, but polished enough to exist in a premium retail setting.

My Contribution

Product to buyer pitch

I contributed to product concepting, packaging directions, physical prototyping, visual merchandising, and the buyer-facing lookbook system.

Product System

Three frame families

The final system organized Wisdom’s eyewear into three retail roles: a flagship statement frame, a sport-driven guard frame, and a more refined everyday frame.

Statement / Face Jewelry

WSDM ONE

A flagship shield frame where the Wisdom wordmark becomes part of the structure. Designed as a statement piece that reads closer to wearable art than traditional eyewear.

Sport / Guard Structure

ARQ Sports / Helmet Frame RB

A sport-driven frame inspired by football helmet language, protective rails, wraparound coverage, and bold colorway energy.

Everyday / Premium

ARQ Daily / Helmet Frame QB

A more restrained frame family that translates the same helmet-inspired DNA into a softer everyday silhouette.

Strategy

Finding Wisdom’s retail position

The brand strategy explored how Wisdom could sit between expressive fashion eyewear and restrained premium frames. That position shaped the form language, material cues, colorways, packaging, and merchandising system.

Process

From visual language to wearable form

The frame development moved between logo-as-structure, shield silhouettes, helmet-inspired guard details, and physical tests. The goal was to make the frames feel like brand objects, not generic sunglasses.

Retail System

Packaging and retail world

The eyewear system expanded into packaging, collector-style cases, social moments, and display concepts that could support a buyer-facing pitch.

Campaign System

Lookbook and campaign direction

The final lookbook brought the product system together through frame introductions, colorways, MSRP and wholesale pricing, moodboards, social strategy, influencer strategy, reel storyboards, packaging, and merchandising.

WISDOM
Final Presentation

From lookbook to client presentation

The final phase of the Wisdom ATL collaboration brought the eyewear system into a client-facing presentation. Teams presented printed lookbooks, physical prototypes, packaging directions, and campaign systems to Wish ATL, showing how the work could live as both a product line and a retail story.

Outcome

Outcome

The project resulted in a retail-facing eyewear system spanning brand strategy, three frame families, physical prototyping, packaging directions, visual merchandising concepts, campaign thinking, lookbook design, and buyer pitch materials.

SCADpro x FIFA World Cup 2026 Atlanta

A UX research and service design collaboration focused on improving the last-mile fan experience, from arrival and wayfinding to venue entry and service support.

Concise case study · Research and service design collaboration

Project Type
UX Research / Service Design
Timeline
2026
Role
UX Research, Journey Mapping, Service Design, Team Collaboration
Tools
Figma, Miro, Research Synthesis, Presentation Design
Overview

Designing for a city-scale event

This SCADpro collaboration explored how fans and visitors might move through Atlanta during FIFA World Cup 2026, with attention to last-mile arrival, wayfinding, accessibility, crowd flow, transportation choices, and venue entry confidence.

The Problem

Reducing friction for visitors

Large-scale event experiences can become confusing quickly, especially when visitors are navigating transportation, crowds, security, and unfamiliar routes at the same time. The project focused on identifying friction points and designing clearer moments of guidance, support, and orientation.

My Role

What I contributed

I contributed to research synthesis, journey mapping, service touchpoint thinking, and presentation materials that translated visitor pain points into clearer opportunity areas for the fan experience.

Key Design Decisions

Mapping the fan journey

  • Map the fan journey across arrival, transportation, last-mile movement, venue entry, and departure
  • Identify service gaps across physical and digital touchpoints
  • Design with crowd flow, accessibility, and visitor confidence in mind
  • Communicate research insights clearly for stakeholders
Visuals

Research artifacts and journey thinking

Outcome

Outcome

The project produced research insights, journey maps, opportunity areas, and service experience recommendations for improving clarity, confidence, and movement across the FIFA World Cup 2026 Atlanta visitor journey.

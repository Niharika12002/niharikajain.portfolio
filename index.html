I'm a UX and graphic designer based in Atlanta, graduating from SCAD in 2025. My work sits at the intersection of clarity and feeling — I want people to navigate experiences without ever feeling lost, and to feel something when they arrive.

Outside of design, I'm usually cooking, discovering music, watching murder mysteries or Bollywood, visiting museums, dressing up, finding Indian homegrown brands, or taking another picture of the sky.

Behind the Build

This portfolio
is also a case study.

"AI proposes. I decide."

I built this portfolio the way I design everything — by directing, deciding, pushing back, and making it feel like me. AI was a tool I used with intention and judgment, not a shortcut. Every prompt was art-directed. Every output was critiqued.

The full record lives on GitHub: every version, every rejected direction, and every moment I said "no, that's not it."

View on GitHub ↗
prompts.md
Prompting as Art Direction
Every AI prompt was a creative brief — written with intent, visual references, and a clear point of view about what I didn't want.
decisions.md
Direction, Not Generation
I made every visual and structural decision. AI generated starting points. I shaped, rejected, and refined until it felt like mine.
rejected-versions.txt
Record of Resistance
I documented every version that felt too generic, too soft, too template-like. Knowing what doesn't feel like you is half the creative brief.
commits.log
Iteration in Public
Every decision is committed. The build history is a design process — public, honest, and ongoing.
